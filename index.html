Temperature quilt

A temperature quilt is a creative and colorful way to document daily temperature changes over a year (or any chosen time period). Each day, you create a small quilt / crochet block using fabrics that represent the high or low temperatures of that day. By the end of the year, you’ll have a vibrant quilt that visually captures the weather patterns of your location.

Typically, quilters assign specific colors to temperature ranges (e.g., blue for cold days, red for hot days) and use these colors to create their blocks. Using this website you can input colors and ranges for 8 color ranges. The design generated will be based on two rows per month, with filler blocks added to even out the months. The resulting design will have 24 rows by 16 columns. Using 3.5 inch blocks would result in a 48 inch wide by 72 inch long block design.
